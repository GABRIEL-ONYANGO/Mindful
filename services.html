What type of therapy are you looking for?

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is one of the most common forms of treatment, and it is often combined with other therapies to treat thoughts to change behaviors. Working one-on-one with our therapist, you will explore negative thoughts that lead to self-destructive feelings and actions.

Motivational Enhancement Therapy

Our therapists use MET to inspire those struggling with addiction to seek treatment. The goal is to help you view life objectively and be real about self-destructive behaviors. Our therapists do not tell you what is wrong and how to change. Instead, they motivate you to take an honest, objective look at your life, leading you to want change.

Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT)

Our therapists will use solution-focused brief therapy (SFBT) to move you closer to positive mental health. The first step is to identify strengths or skills already acquired that can help you overcome obstacles.

Trauma Therapy

Traumatic experiences can hinder emotional development. And until the trauma is processed, emotional development remains stagnant. That’s where trauma therapy helps. Traumas can include fighting in a war, surviving natural disasters, sexual or physical abuse, and even losing a loved one.

Interpersonal Therapy

How you interact with the people in your life can sometimes be fragmented. Interpersonal therapy helps you overcome the obstacles in your relationships by improving communication skills, conflict resolution, and coping skills for difficult and unexpected times, like when you experience a loss.
